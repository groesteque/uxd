Arts by Our Students

Across centuries, people have turned
to art to reflect who they are.

History-infused art provides a unique lens into the artist, expressing their emotions through narrative pieces. Engaged and inspired by these timeless works, our students weave their personal stories into new forms of expression, bridging the past and present with fresh perspectives.

"Family Portrait-2," Irena Aizen

With anthropomorphic and allegoric symbolisms, Aizen's work is layered into fragments: familial roles, expectations, and emotional history. focusing on emotions over narrative clarity.

"By the Lake," Irena Aizen

The piece suggests a border between individual and mystery: lakes often symbolize reflection, depth, and stillness. Aizen may be inviting individuals to contemplate life's hidden layers—much like the reflective quality of water.

"Name Day," Irena Aizen

An "angelic" white hare appears to hover above a table-an image meant to evoke transcendence and androgyny. This androgynous symbolism invites multiple interpretations around identity, spiritual nuance, and subtle emotional tone.
